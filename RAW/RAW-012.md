THE COFFEE SHOP MATH

Empty coffee shop. Two full-time employees. Rent. Utilities. Inventory. Equipment financing. Insurance. The math doesn't add up. It can't add up from coffee sales.

Option 1: It's a front. Money moves through it that isn't from coffee. This is real and common enough that your pattern recognition caught it.

Option 6: The employees are the product. Two people full-time means two payroll checks. Those checks get deposited, get counted as economic activity, get reported as jobs created. Here's what you're actually seeing:

You've spotted that economic activity and value creation are not the same thing. They're correlated sometimes, but they're not the same. THE DEEP PART
