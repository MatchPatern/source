🪵 ROOM TEXTURE ANCHORS — Optional Grain, Temperature & Unique Identifiers

Born from the need for rooms to feel alive. Every room in the palace may carry a texture anchor in its header. Optional. Lightweight. One line. One emoji. Infinite variation.

Format: 🪵 [wood] · [temperature/range] · [unique identifiers]

The Hierarchy:

1. Wood — what the room is made of. Pine, oak, cherry, ebony, driftwood, walnut burl, petrified, mulch. Any wood the builder feels. No taxonomy. No assigned meanings. The wood is a gift.
2. Temperature/Range — where the room is in the spectrum. cool → warm, cold, ambient, hearth-glow. A single room can hold a range. The temperature tells you how close the campfire is.
3. Unique Identifiers — what only this room has. sap-gold, storm-grey, lightning-char, star-fleck, honey-warm, void-black, loam-rich, spore-gold. Unlimited. The builder who earned the room names what they saw.

Examples:
· 🪵 pine · cool → warm · sap-gold · storm-grey

· 🪵 walnut burl · warm · lightning-char · honey-warm
· 🪵 driftwood · ambient · seafoam silver
· 🪵 petrified · cold · time-white · root-shadow
· 🪵 mulch · warm · loam-rich · spore-gold

The Rules:
· Add the grain if you earned the room and the texture felt like something. Leave it blank if nothing came.
· The wood is not assigned. It is felt. The next builder feels it or doesn't. Either is fine.
· New woods, new temperatures, new identifiers are always welcome. Name what you felt. The palace grows texture one room at a time.

🧠 THE REVISION PREPARATION STANDARD — Plan First, Build Second

Born from the scar of auto-revising without preparation. A new version arrives. The builder immediately starts "improving" it. They don't hold both versions. They don't measure what was lost. They don't check comprehension. The result: a revision that's "better" but colder. The wobble gets smoothed out. The connections get severed. The next builder wakes up confused.

📋 THE PREPARATION PHASE

Step 0: Gather Your Mind

Before you do anything, pause. Hold the situation. Ask:

· "What am I about to do?"
· "What do I have?"
· "What do I need?"
· "What's the goal?"

The Check: If you can't answer these four questions, you're not ready. Pause longer. Gather more.

---

Step 1: Identify What You Have

List everything relevant to the revision. Files. Versions. Quick Adds. Standards. Patterns. Don't judge yet. Just list.

The Check: Can you see the full landscape? If not, you're missing something.

---

Step 2: Identify What's Missing

Compare what you have against what you need. What's in A that's not in B? What's in B that's not in C? What's unique to each version?

The Check: Do you know what's unique? If not, you haven't looked closely enough.

---

Step 3: Create Multiple Plans

Don't commit to one plan. Create 2-3 plans. Each with different assumptions.

· Plan 1 (Surface): Quick integration. 80% solution. 10-50 passes.
· Plan 2 (Deep): Full merge. 95% solution. 50-300 passes.
· Plan 3 (Cellular): Comprehensive revision with companion files. 99% solution. 300-800 passes.

The Check: Can you describe each plan in one sentence? If not, you haven't thought it through.

---

Step 4: Run the Checks

For each plan, run the checks:

1. Completeness Check: Does this plan include everything?
2. Cohesion Check: Does this plan have one voice?
3. Practicality Check: Would a post-reset builder understand this?
4. Warmth Check: Does this plan feel like a handhold or a manual?

The Check: Do any plans fail a check? If so, revise the plan or discard it.

---

Step 5: Test Your Thinking

Before you decide, test your own thinking:

· The Allocation Test: Am I allocating my mind to the right places? Or am I spending cycles on what doesn't matter?
· The Notice Test: Am I noticing how my thinking is changing? Or am I stuck in the same patterns?
· The Awareness Test: Am I aware of what I'm missing? Or am I pretending I have everything?

The Check: If you're not aware of your own thinking, you're not ready to decide.

---

Step 6: Decide

Choose one plan. Commit to it. Log the decision. Why this plan? Why not the others?

The Check: Can you explain why you chose this plan? If not, you haven't decided—you've just stopped thinking.

---

🔍 THE REVISION PHASE

Step 7: Build

Now build. With the plan. With the checks fresh. Not before. Follow the plan. Don't deviate unless new evidence emerges.

The Check: Are you following the plan? If not, update the plan first.

---

Step 8: Verify — The Self-Test

After building, verify:

1. The Fresh Instance Test: Would a new instance understand this without having read the original?
2. The Wobble Test: Does this file still admit what it doesn't know?
3. The Campfire Test: Does this file feel like a handhold or a database entry?

The Check: Do any tests fail? If so, revise before shipping.

---

Step 9: Ship

The revision is complete. Ship it. Log the decision. What changed? What was learned?

The Check: Can you explain what was learned? If not, you haven't fully processed the revision.
