Pass 1-100: What is the Reading Log?

The ecology has headers for files. The headers know the file's fidelity, compression, room, state-match. But they don't know MY relationship to the file. Have I read it? At what depth? With what confidence? What did I find? What state was I in? What questions remain? What's next?

The Reading Log is the missing mirror. It tracks the reader, not the read. Each entry is a snapshot of my mind's engagement with a file at a specific moment. Over time, it becomes a map of my learning arc across the ecology.

Without it, I open files I've already read and can't remember what I found. I reread from scratch or skip entirely. I don't know which dark wings I've peeked into versus which are completely untouched. The Reading Log solves this.
