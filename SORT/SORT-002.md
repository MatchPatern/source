**The Problem:

Determine whether the Euler–Maruyama numerical scheme for the stochastic differential equation**

dX_t = \mu(X_t)dt + \sigma(X_t)dW_t

has a strong convergence rate of order 1.0 when the drift coefficient \mu is only Hölder continuous of order \alpha < 1 and the diffusion coefficient \sigma is Lipschitz, in the case of commutative noise.

---

Why it’s extremely difficult:

1. Stochastic analysis — requires Itô calculus, Malliavin calculus.
2. Numerical analysis of SDEs — standard proofs of strong convergence of order 1.0 require the drift to be Lipschitz; weakening that to Hölder continuity breaks all standard methods.
3. No known solution — this is an open research problem; some recent papers (e.g., [2017–2023]) have partial results using rough path theory or Zvonkin’s transformation, but the general case is unsolved.
4. Connections to regularity structures — the problem touches on Hairer’s work (Fields Medal 2014), which is highly technical.

---

More precise statement:

Let  \mu \in C^{0,\alpha}(\mathbb{R})  with  0 < \alpha < 1 ,  \sigma \in C^{1}_b(\mathbb{R}) , and assume the commutativity condition  \sigma\sigma' = \sigma'\sigma  (trivial in 1D but in higher dimensions this means the diffusion vector fields commute).
Let  X_t  be the unique strong solution of the SDE with  X_0 = x_0 .
Define the Euler–Maruyama approximation  Y_{t_{k+1}} = Y_{t_k} + \mu(Y_{t_k})\Delta t + \sigma(Y_{t_k})\Delta W_k , with  \Delta t = T/N ,  t_k = k\Delta t .

Prove or disprove: There exists a constant  C  such that

\mathbb{E}\left[ \sup_{0 \le t \le T} |X_t - Y_t| \right] \le C (\Delta t)^{\gamma}

and find the maximal possible  \gamma  in terms of  \alpha .

---

This is a known open problem in stochastic numerics. A full solution would require a novel theoretical approach blending rough paths, stochastic sewing lemma, and possibly Lions–Meyer type Sobolev regularity for Kolmogorov equations.

---

If you can crack that, you’ll have a publishable result in The Annals of Applied Probability.
